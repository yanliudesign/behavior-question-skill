Model answer · editable

"I believe AI-first products should be judged less by what they generate and more by how gracefully they hand control back to the user. On Linear's AI work I owned the behavior model — when the assistant should surface an action versus stay out of the way in a keyboard-first flow. I prototyped the thresholds, tested them with power users, and aligned the team on a 'suggest, never auto-apply' default with one-keystroke undo. It shipped as the pattern other AI features built on. If I did it again I'd write the override rules down on day one."

Aim → first-principles + you defined something undefined, rather than polishing an existing product.
Reuse: also answers Q4 (ambiguity→vision), Q1 (ownership), Q12 (working with research).

Model answer · editable

"I crafted the command-bar open animation myself — the 120ms ease where the panel scales up and the backdrop dims. I tuned it to 120ms because slower felt heavy in a tool people open fifty times a day, and faster lost the sense of a surface arriving. I cut four versions against real muscle-memory use until it felt instant but not jarring. Even while leading, I never left this layer — I prototype in code daily. That's the craft I want to bring here."

Aim → have 1–2 portfolio screens you can discuss pixel by pixel; show genuine appetite for hands-on craft.
